What is the NFA?

A Nondeterministic Finite Automaton models life's uncertainty. From any state you may follow multiple possible transitions for the same choice - or slide silently through an ε (epsilon) transition without deciding anything at all. The machine exists in all possible futures simultaneously, which is exactly how real decisions feel: you don't know which branch you're on until it's too late.

What is the DFA?

A Deterministic Finite Automaton is what fate looks like in hindsight. Using subset construction, each NFA state-set collapses into a single deterministic node. Epsilon ambiguity is absorbed. Branching is resolved. The result: one path, one outcome - no matter how many choices felt available. States shown in {braces} are merged NFA states, proving that your apparent freedom was always just one machine running in parallel.

NFA vs DFA

The NFA (left) shows the raw nondeterminism of life - multiple transitions per symbol, silent ε-moves, states that blur into one another. The DFA (right) is the mathematical truth: every NFA has an equivalent DFA, built by tracking all possible NFA states simultaneously. The chaos on the left and the clarity on the right describe exactly the same language - the same set of decision sequences that end in death. Free will is the NFA. Fate is the DFA.
